Our Program

What We Offer

Pillars of Learning

Our program thoughtfully integrates language, culture, science, and hands-on discovery to support the whole child — academically, socially, and emotionally.

🌱

Montessori at the Core

Our classrooms follow time-tested Montessori principles that honor each child's individual pace and potential. Children are guided by carefully prepared environments where hands-on materials move from concrete to abstract thinking — building confidence, concentration, and intrinsic motivation.

  • Child-led, self-directed learning
  • Mixed-age classrooms (18 months–6 years)
  • Individualized lessons and pacing
  • Grace & courtesy woven into daily life
🌍

Bilingual Language Immersion

Children are naturally immersed in both Mandarin and English throughout their day. Language is not taught in isolation — it is lived through interaction, stories, songs, and daily communication.

  • Daily Mandarin & English communication
  • Songs, stories, and circle time in both languages
  • Rich vocabulary through real-world experiences
  • Cultural appreciation woven into daily life
🔬

STEM-Inspired Discovery

Science, technology, engineering, and math come alive through hands-on Montessori work — observing nature, building, sorting, measuring, and asking "why?". STEM is woven into the prepared environment, not taught as a separate subject.

  • Nature study, observation & simple experiments
  • Building, sorting & pattern work with Montessori materials
  • Math grounded in concrete, real-world materials
  • Curiosity-driven questions and inquiry
💛

Whole-Child Development

At Mega, education is more than academics. We foster social-emotional learning, grace and courtesy, respect, responsibility, and positive communication every day. Children feel safe to explore, grow, and develop into compassionate, confident global citizens.

  • Social-emotional learning environment
  • Emotional regulation & conflict resolution
  • Collaboration and community building
  • Calm, joyful, and safe classroom atmosphere
"Educating the whole child — mind, character, and voice."
— Maria Montessori
Inside the Classroom

Classroom Areas

Every Montessori classroom is organized into four core areas of learning. Each area is carefully designed with hands-on materials that invite exploration, build skills, and foster independence. Cultural and science work — geography, biology, and simple experiments — extends these areas with STEM-inspired exploration.

🫖

Practical Life Area

Activities: Water pouring, sweeping, dressing frames, food preparation.

Materials: Child-sized brooms, pitchers, utensils, dressing frames.

Benefits: Develops fine motor skills, independence, concentration, and a sense of responsibility.

📖

Language Area

Activities: Sandpaper letters, movable alphabet, word building, storytelling.

Materials: Sandpaper letters, language cards, storybooks, moveable alphabets.

Benefits: Enhances vocabulary, communication skills, reading and writing readiness, and cognitive development.

🔢

Mathematics Area

Activities: Number rods, spindle boxes, bead chains, golden beads.

Materials: Bead frames, number rods, sandpaper numbers, math games.

Benefits: Builds a strong foundation in counting, addition, subtraction, and logical thinking.

🎨

Sensorial Area

Activities: Pink tower, color tablets, sound cylinders, fabric boxes.

Materials: Geometric solids, smelling bottles, tasting jars, baric tablets.

Benefits: Enhances sensory perception, cognitive development, language skills, and critical thinking.
